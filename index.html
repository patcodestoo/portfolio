About the Project

The the following case study shows the process on what I would do if I was set to redesign BitGive's website and visual identity. Currently, the project consists of a new logo, a Design system and a mockup website landing page.

Challenges

BitGive needed a simple yet bold, consistent visual identity that seized the user's attention. Their main website needed a visual upgrade. I found that BitGive requires a visual language that could amplify its goals and vision of a future in which BlockChain Charity could become mainstream as a Transparent, efficient and elegant method to deliver help to populations in need worldwide.

StyleScape

To make BitGive's vision consistent and memorable, two visual strategies needed to be implemented; a Stylescape/Moodboard and a Design System. The Stylescape's goal was to capture a mood, a personality, and a voice for the brand. Out of a list of possible attributes to define the BitGive brand, the following was chosen as the most representative; clean, bold, and nurturing.

These attributes will help visualize the BitGive Brand, bringing it more to an organic feel, where curves and patterns would make the human element paramount. Avatars of the brand were created to illustrate the target supporter of BitGive. I imagined someone from the finance world with Blockchain technology know-how, who could empathize with the people BitGive helps.

The Stylescape's layout was made in Illustrator, and the color correction was made in Photoshop.

From Top to Bottom
The Khadija Stylescape: Clean, serious and professional look focused on human photography.
The John Stylescape: A happy medium, bold and centered on organic graphic shapes.
The Claire Stylescape: Adventurous, trendy and forward thinking. Ideal for NFT projects.

Logo

The new logo needed to represent BitGive's worldwide reach and holistic purpose of making the world a better place with technology.

Most of the Mockups were done in Photoshop and Illustrator.

BitGive Logo

Design System

To have a consistent visual identity, BitGive required a design system. The elements were custom and designed in Figma, Illustrator and Photoshop.

Spacing and Layout

Layout is the base structure for our visual identity. Most of the layout design was done with autolayout in Figma with a 8px base spacing. All tokens and layout elements are multiples of eight and set to double,quadruple and octuple times the base 1rem (16px) token.
